## 📥 Mandatory Submissions
To be eligible for judging, participants must submit the following:
1.  **GitHub Link:** The complete source code of the project.
2.  **Deployment:** A live link to the working application.
3.  **Pitch Deck:** Presentation slides intended for the judges.
4.  **Demo Video:** A recording that showcases the project in action.

## ⚖️ Hackathon Judging Rubric
**Total Score:** 100 Points (Up to 110 with Bonus)

### Core Judging Criteria
Projects will be judged on the following foundational pillars:
* **Usefulness:** Does the solution solve a real-world problem?
* **Creativity:** Is the approach novel and original?
* **AI Integration:** Is AI a core part of the solution, rather than just superficial?
* **Execution:** Is there a complete and working demo?

### Detailed Scoring Breakdown
The core criteria are further classified into the following point system:

#### 1. Functionality & AI Usage (40 Points)
* **App Core Functionality (30 pts):** Evaluates if the application works as intended without major bugs.
* **AI Integration (10 pts):** Evaluates if AI is meaningfully integrated into the solution rather than being superficial.

#### 2. Creativity & Problem-Solving (30 Points)
* **Problem Impact (20 pts):** Assesses if the project is solving a well-defined, real-world problem.
* **Technical Excellence (10 pts):** Assessment of code quality, logic, and overall system architecture.

#### 3. Presentation & Demo (20 Points)
* **Pitch Clarity (10 pts):** Based on a 3-minute pitch that tells a clear, compelling story of the project.
* **Demo Quality (10 pts):** Evaluates a live or recorded demo that successfully showcases all key features.

#### 4. Execution Bonus (10 Points)
* **Efficiency (10 pts):** Awarded for an exceptionally polished or complex build completed within the 18-hour timeline.
* *Note to Participants:* While the base total is 100 points, this bonus allows top-tier teams to reach a maximum of 110 points.
